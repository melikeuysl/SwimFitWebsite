Free Style Swimming

Freestyle is a swimming category that allows swimmers to use any stroke they prefer. However, most swimmers use the front crawl technique because it is the fastest and most efficient.

Advantages of Freestyle Swimming:

  1. It is the fastest swimming technique
  2. It is less tiring in the water.
  3. Easy to learn and improves breathing control.
  4. Provides a full-body workout.

Freestyle Swimming Techniques

  • Body Position

    • Keep your body horizontal and aligned with the water.Your head should be slightly tilted downward to keep your neck relaxed.Your hips and legs should stay close to the water surface.




  • Arm Movements (Pull and Push Phase)

    • Your hand should enter the water in line with your shoulder and slightly bent.Pull your hand back toward your stomach.Extend your hand backward to push the water.Lift your arm out of the water and bring it forward again.




  • Kicking Technique (Flutter Kick)

    • Keep your legs straight, just below the surface of the water. Keep your legs straight, just below the surface of the water.Keep your ankles relaxed and kick rhythmically.




  • Breathing Technique

    • Turn your head to the right or left to take a breath (learning to breathe on both sides is important).Do not lift your head too much while breathing, as it will disturb your balance.Exhale underwater and inhale when your face is above water.



Freestyle Training Plan

Set Distance Focus Intensity
1 200m Warm-up (Easy pace freestyle) Low
2 100m Freestyle with bilateral breathing Medium
3 50m Freestyle sprint High
4 200m Cool-down freestyle Low
