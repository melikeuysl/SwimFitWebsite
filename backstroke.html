BackStroke Swimming

Backstroke is the only competitive stroke swum on the back. It allows for continuous breathing but requires excellent coordination of arm movements and a strong flutter kick. Proper body position, efficient rotation, and controlled breathing are essential for improving speed and endurance in backstroke.

Advantages of BackStroke Swimming:

  1. Easy Breathing: Your face stays above water, allowing continuous breathing.
  2. Better Posture: Strengthens back and core muscles, improving posture.
  3. Safer Swimming: Reduces the risk of drowning as you float on your back.
  4. Balanced Muscles: Works back muscles, preventing muscle imbalances.

BackStroke Swimming Techniques

  • Body Position

    • Keep your body flat and as close to the water surface as possible.Keep your head still and look straight up at the ceiling.A slight body rotation helps generate more power and efficiency.




  • Arm Movements

    • Perform a continuous and alternating motion, ensuring full extension and a powerful pull.




  • Kicking Technique

    • Use a steady flutter kick to maintain propulsion and balance.




  • Breathing Technique

    • Since your face is above water, maintain a steady breathing rhythm.
