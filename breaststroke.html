Breaststroke Swimming

Breaststroke is one of the most popular swimming styles, known for its gliding motion and strong leg kick. It requires good coordination between the arms, legs, and breathing. This stroke is ideal for endurance and beginners due to its controlled pace.

Advantages of Breaststroke Swimming:

  1. Easy to Learn: It has a slow and controlled rhythm.
  2. Great for Endurance: Uses less energy compared to other strokes.
  3. Works the Legs: Strengthens thighs and glutes with the frog kick.
  4. Good for Safety: Allows swimmers to keep their head above water.

Breaststroke Swimming Techniques

  • Body Position

    • Keep your body as flat as possible, slightly inclined forward.


  • Arm Movements

    • Perform a wide scoop motion and glide between strokes. Proper coordination of arm pull, leg kick, and glide is crucial.




  • Kicking Technique

    • Use a powerful frog kick for propulsion.




  • Breathing Technique

    • Inhale during the pull phase and exhale when gliding.



Breaststroke Training Plan

Set Distance Focus Intensity
1 200m Warm-up (Smooth breaststroke) Low
2 100m Breaststroke with longer glides Medium
